Worm Rearing Guide

4th Stage

Feed the worms on the entire shoots for 6 days.

Molting

As the worms enter the next instar, their skin will shed, which is known as molting. Molting takes 18-24 hours (1 day), and during this period, the worms should not be fed.

Besides counting the days, the rearer should be able to identify when the worms are going in and out of molting.

Signs of Molting:

  • Swollen heads
  • Heads raised
  • Worms remain still

During molting, keep the bedding dry and open.

Signs of Coming Out of Molting:

  • Worms are active and move around
  • Mouthparts are wide
  • Body is soft with loose skin
  • Shed skins are easily visible on the bed

After the worms have come out of molting, spread them evenly to increase the dry matter in the bed and adjust the space according to their growing body size.

Feed the worms after they all have come out of molting.
