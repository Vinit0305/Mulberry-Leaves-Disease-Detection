Worm Rearing Guide

Phase 1

Young worms should be fed young tender leaves; 2nd and 3rd leaves come from the tips of shoots. These are cut into small pieces and fed to the worms at least twice a day, in the morning and afternoon, for 4 days. After 4 days, the worms go into sleep.

Molting

Molting is when the worms shed their skin as they enter the next instar. Molting takes 18-24 hours (1 day), and during this period, the worms should not be fed.

Apart from counting the days, the rearer should be able to identify when the worms enter and exit the molt.

Signs of Molting:

  • Swollen heads
  • Heads held high
  • Worms remain still

During molting, the bedding should be kept dry and open.

Signs of Coming Out of Molt:

  • Worms are active and move around
  • The mouthpart is enlarged
  • The body is elongated with loose skin
  • Shedded skins are easily visible on the bedding

After the worms emerge from the molt, spread them evenly on the bedding to increase dryness and to accommodate their growing body size.

Once all the worms are out of the molt, feed them.
