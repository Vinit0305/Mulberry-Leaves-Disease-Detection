Worm Rearing Guide

Stage 3

Continue feeding the silkworms with good leaves harvested from the green parts of the shoots for three days. After this period, the silkworms will enter into molt.

Molting

When the worms enter the next instar, their skin sheds, which is known as molting. Molting takes 18-24 hours (1 day), and during this time, the worms should not be fed.

Instead of just counting the days, the farmer should be able to recognize when the worms enter and exit the molt.

Signs of Molting:

  • Swollen heads
  • Raised heads
  • The worms remain motionless

During molting, the bed should be kept dry and open.

Signs of Emerging from Molt:

  • The worms are active and moving around
  • The mouthpart is enlarged
  • The body appears translucent with loose skin
  • Shed skins are easily visible on the bed

After the worms emerge from the molt, spread them evenly to increase the bed space to accommodate their growing body size and to enhance dryness on the bed.

Once all the worms have emerged from the molt, feed them.
