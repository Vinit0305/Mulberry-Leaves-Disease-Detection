Worm Rearing Guide

Phase 2

Feed the worms with fresh leaves from the 3rd and 4th buds. Continue feeding the cut leaves for 4 days. After this period, the worms will enter molting again.

Molting

Molting is when the worms shed their skin as they transition to the next stage. Molting takes 18-24 hours (1 day), and during this period, the worms should not be fed.

The caretaker must be able to identify when the worms are entering and exiting molting, rather than just counting the days.

Signs of Molting:

  • Swollen heads
  • Raised heads
  • Worms remain still

During molting, the bedding should be kept dry and open.

Signs of Exiting Molting:

  • Worms become active and move around
  • The mouth area is widened
  • The body appears thin with loose skin
  • Shed skins are easily visible on the bedding

After the worms exit molting, spread them out evenly to increase dryness in the bedding and adjust the space to accommodate their growing body size.

Feed them once all worms have exited molting.
